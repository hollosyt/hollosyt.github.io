Our Services

DeepFeet Ashiatsu Bar Therapy

Deep Tissue Without Discomfort

Ashiatsu is a barefoot massage technique where the therapist uses overhead bars for balance and support while delivering deep, broad, consistent pressure with the feet. This technique provides a uniquely deep yet comfortable experience — the broad surface of the foot allows for greater pressure without the sharp discomfort of elbows or thumbs.

Swedish & Hot Stone Massage

Our Signature Therapeutic Experience

A therapeutic session tailored to suit your needs — the best choice for youth, elderly, medically fragile, or anyone seeking pure relaxation.

Black Rock Bodywork is named for the basalt stones utilized in hot stone massage. Combining the therapeutic heat of smooth basalt stones with gentle massage techniques creates deep relaxation and relieves muscle tension. Heated stones are massaged over your entire body, helping to warm and loosen tight muscles. The direct heat enhances blood flow, which promotes better circulation, aiding muscle recovery.

Benefits include easing stress, reducing chronic pain, and improving sleep. The heat penetrates deeply, relaxing muscles more effectively than traditional massage, making it ideal for those with back pain, arthritis, or muscle stiffness.

AromaTouch Technique

A clinical-grade essential oil application on the back and feet that promotes deep relaxation, reduces physical and emotional stress, and supports immune health. By using specific, layered oils, this non-invasive technique balances the nervous system, eases tension, and enhances overall well-being.

About

Meet Samantha

Samantha is a Licensed Massage Therapist trained at Lansing Community College. She brings both clinical knowledge and a deep appreciation for the science of how the body functions at its best. She specializes in Ashiatsu barefoot massage and Swedish massage, using a personalized, targeted approach to address areas of pain and tension while promoting deep relaxation.

Before transitioning into massage therapy, Sam earned both bachelor's and master's degrees in botany and plant pathology from Michigan State University and spent years working in a research environment. Her background in science continues to shape her practice, guiding her understanding of anatomy, physiology, and the body's natural healing processes.

Her approach centers on building trust and rapport with each client. By listening carefully and tailoring every session to individual needs, she strives to facilitate healing, reduce tension, and create a restorative experience that supports overall well-being.
